The opportunity

Today, referrers pick a center the way they did in 2005.

They keep a printed list, ask the front desk who they trust, or guess. Patients end up at out-of-network, low-quality, or far-away centers — and Expert Radiology's network captures less of the referral flow than the brand and quality should command.

Today

  • Outdated lists, paper rolodexes, word-of-mouth
  • No visibility into turnaround, capacity, or accreditation
  • Patients no-show after long-distance referrals
  • Referrers can't tell ER partners from generic centers
  • Zero signal back: did the referral happen? Did it land?

What we're building

  • A modern directory referrers actually want to open
  • Live ranking by distance, quality, and network value
  • Transparent score with auditable factors — no black box
  • ER partner uplift surfaces routing opportunities
  • Every search becomes a referral attribution signal

Working prototype

What's live today.

Built end-to-end as a working web app. Real ranking logic, real filters, real map. Mock data for 20 imaging centers across NYC, Houston, and Chicago — diverse enough that toggling weights or filters visibly re-orders the list.

Search by location

Type a zip, neighborhood, or city. The map and list re-center instantly. Quick-pick chips for fast demos.

Composite ranking

Six factors blended into one 0–100 score: distance, quality, turnaround, cost, availability, and network value.

Tunable weights

Live sliders let providers (or our team) see why the rank is what it is. The model is auditable, not a black box.

Clinical filters

Distance radius, state, ACR accreditation seals, subspecialty, walk-ins, on-site radiologist. Active filters render as removable chips.

Detail panel

Center hero, score breakdown, accreditations strip, modalities, insurance, contact, and a "Send referral" CTA.

Live, mobile-ready

Deployed to production. Full-bleed Google-Maps-style layout on desktop; stacks gracefully on mobile.

How the recommendation works

Six factors. One transparent score.

Each center gets a 0–100 composite from six weighted sub-scores. The weights are tunable in the UI so the reviewer can see the model react. Clinicians won't trust a black box; they'll adopt a model whose math they can audit.

Distance
Great-circle miles from patient
Adherence drops fast with travel time
Quality
Patient rating × review volume
Stars alone are noisy at low N
Turnaround
Median scan-to-final-report hours
Drives provider satisfaction & re-referral
Cost
Estimated patient out-of-pocket
Affordability and value-based-care signals
Availability
Hours until next open appointment
Same-week beats higher-quality-but-3-weeks-out

12-month roadmap

From prototype to network compounding.

Trust beats features. Two-sided liquidity beats single-sided delight. Closing the referral loop beats adding ranking factors. The plan reflects those priorities.

Phase 1
0–30 DAYS
Foundation & data trust. One design partner using it daily.
  • Real center data, one metro pilot
  • Replace mock geocoder with Mapbox
  • Live availability ingest from RamSoft (PACS)
  • Auth with NPI verification
  • Ship to one PI law firm or chiropractor practice
Phase 2
31–90 DAYS
Ranking quality + closing the referral loop.
  • "Send referral" via fax / SMART-on-FHIR
  • Personalized weights from provider history
  • Subspecialty matching (neuro vs MSK readers)
  • Insurance match via payer eligibility APIs
  • Provider attribution: source → choice → attendance
  • Center self-serve admin
Phase 3
91–180 DAYS
Two-sided value. Centers see this as an acquisition channel.
  • Imaging center dashboard (referrals, win/loss, funnel)
  • Patient-side companion (extends the existing portal)
  • SMART-on-FHIR launch from Epic / Cerner
  • No Surprises Act / payer transparency estimates
Phase 4
181–365 DAYS
AI-native referral intelligence at national scale.
  • LLM that reads referring notes → modality + subspecialty
  • Outcomes-based ranking from PrecisionPlus signals
  • Pre-auth assistance auto-drafted from notes
  • National multi-region tenancy
  • HIPAA / HITRUST / SOC 2 readiness
Gate to ship past day 90: time-to-choice under 90 seconds, referral completion ≥ 30%, and partner-network share ≥ 55%. If we don't measurably move those, the model isn't ready.

Honest about what could go wrong

Risks & dependencies.

We won't pretend these don't exist. Each has an explicit mitigation; nothing here is a blocker, but each needs an owner.

Data freshness on non-partner centers

Capacity and insurance change weekly. Stale data poisons trust. Mitigation: surface "last updated" prominently; combine scrape + payer feeds + center self-serve; degrade gracefully.

RamSoft / PACS dependency

Live availability for partner centers depends on their cooperation. Mitigation: queue + cache fallback; don't block our roadmap on theirs.

HIPAA scope creep

Once patient names enter referrals, every system the data touches becomes regulated. Mitigation: decide compliance posture before features, not after.

Provider attribution leakage

Referrers will pick up the phone instead of clicking. Mitigation: SMS-back-to-patient confirmation gives a separate attendance signal even when click attribution misses.

Geocoding vendor lock-in

Mapbox / Google billing scales nonlinearly. Mitigation: aggressive caching; keep the interface vendor-agnostic from day one.

Distribution

A directory nobody knows about is a search box. Mitigation: tie launches to existing PI-attorney and pain-management channels — those are ER's hottest wedges today.
